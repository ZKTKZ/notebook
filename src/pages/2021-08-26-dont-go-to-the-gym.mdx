# You don't have to go the gym
You don't have to go to the gym to be active. [^6]

[5 minutes of exercise](https://well.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/05/09/the-scientific-7-minute-workout/)(especially when it is high-intensity) is much better than 0 minutes of exercise.

It is not a linear curve.
It is a ...


Build pockets of fitness into your space. This lets you be healthy without thinking. No willpower needed.

(https://www.nutritiousmovement.com/dynamic-at-home-reaching-and-hanging-spaces/).


Here are a few toys to get you started::
- Yoga mat rug ($40) [^7]
- Pullup bar ($50)
- Balance board ($30)
- Skipping rope (N/A)

If you are frugal, you can still get by.

For 24 hours, use only your legs to turn lights on/off. I recommend wall dips[https://medium.com/parkouredu/parkour-strength-training-for-beginners-949e961129b9] with the cabinet for upper-body strength.

You'll have to make adjustments based on what kind of space you're in.  I live in an apartment, so I can't build a rock climbing wall in my garage. I also can't keep the pullup bar in my doorway overnight.[^8].
